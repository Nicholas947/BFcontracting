DELIVERY METHODS

DELIVERING SUCCESS

At B&F Contracting, Inc. we understand the importance of choosing the right contract delivery method for your project. Different methods offer diverse benefits and drawbacks, and we have the expertise to help determine the best approach for your specific needs.

When considering the different procurement methods it is important to always keep in mind “Best Value” which is a combination of price, quality schedule and most importantly a team effort delivering to the owner the best of all these various facets of the project. The owner would typically determine best value which may be unique from one owner and project to the next. Low price may be a high priority or possibly an accelerated schedule along with quality could be more important.

Design Bid Build

Sometimes referred to as “Hard Bid” or “Low Bid” is a common and popular procurement approach. With this method, the owner hires a designer to create plans and specifications, and then invites contractors to bid on the project. Typically, pricing on the job is submitted confidentially and the lowest priced bidder expects to get the contract. The DBB method is straightforward and allows for competitive bidding but may result in potential conflicts between the designer and contractor. There is a possibility for discrepancies in the plans and specifications which may result in the contractor requesting more money once the selected. This occurs through change orders, which result in a bid price higher than the original submittal. Additionally, bidders on publicly funded projects are usually required to supply a bid bond, performance bond, and/or payment bond to guarantee their company’s financial stability. The upside of DBB often is it brings the lowest price. This allows the owner to save on costs and it’s especially beneficial on projects where taxpayer funds are involved. Public agencies are obligated to award the lowest responsive bidder in most cases. On the downside, the lowest bidder often is not the most experienced. Since the contractor is selected solely on price, they may not provide the highest quality, safety, innovative methods, and Best Value. B&F has extensive experience with DBB projects and can help navigate potential issues that may arise both prior to the bid date and after the bid. Even though it is not required in this delivery method, B&F will still provide constructability input as well as budget, schedule, design, and potential value engineering alternatives. These are just a few strategies B&F implements which can add value to your project.

JOC

JOC contracts allow for the completion of multiple, small to medium-sized projects within a single procurement contract. This streamlined process saves time, reduces administrative burden, and leads to cost savings. JOCs are generally advertised publicly with parameters set on single job limits, cumulative job limits, per year and advertised scope of work. Under a JOC contract, a pre-qualified contractor or multiple contractors (generally around 3) are selected to complete a variety of construction projects. These projects may arise for capital improvement or operations and maintenance. Typically, these are a one-year contract with annual extensions of anywhere between one year to five years, but it may vary. As the owner agency, JOCs allow one contract to be approved which can have multiple smaller contracts not subject to going through the approval process again. B&F likes to work directly with the owner to determine goals including budget, schedule, quality expectations, community impact, aesthetics, lifespan, etc. This collaboration between the contractor and the owner results in projects being completed on time, on budget, and to the satisfaction of all parties involved. JOC contracts have become the go-to procurement method for government agencies and organizations that require frequent construction projects. If you're looking for a procurement method on smaller projects that are efficient, cost-effective, and promote collaboration, JOCs may be the procurement method you are looking for. At B&F, we are experts at managing multiple job orders concurrently and have active JOCs with agencies all over Arizona. Our unmatched expertise, quality, and self-performing ability allow us to respond quickly and efficiently to our clients’ job orders.

CMAR

This procurement method brings the contractor in early in the process where the project may still be in the conceptual and design phase. The owner benefits from the contractor’s expertise and input on the design and construction aspects. CMAR is a collaborative approach that results in a more efficient and cost-effective construction process, with reduced risk of change orders and delays. This delivery method offers unmatched benefits to owners, including increased collaboration, reduced risk, and improved project outcomes. Contractors are chosen based on their qualifications, which spans from their experience with similar projects to the overall value they can provide to the project. With the construction manager assuming the risk for the construction process, owners can have peace of mind knowing that their project is in the most capable hands. CMAR contracts are the perfect solution for projects that require a high level of collaboration and risk management, which in many cases makes them the ultimate procurement method for your construction project! B&F is proud to have worked on many successful CMAR projects. Our expertise and proven CMAR approach will provide valuable insight to your project, ensuring it is completed on time and within budget.
